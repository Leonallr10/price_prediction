Trending

Price prediction
Increasing prices
Shortages of components and higher production costs (due to inflation) are forcing manufacturers to increase prices, shifting the cost increment to consumers . With sales stagnating, most manufacturers are also trying to move up the value chain and focus on increasing their revenue and margins by focusing on mid-range and higher-end models rather than entry-level models with lower margins, which is another factor driving the general increase in average selling prices.
Prediction
Market leaders putting a stranglehold on competition
During the pandemic, market leaders like Samsung and Apple saw their market shares rise, a trend expected to continue. Their dominance affords them greater influence over suppliers due to economies of scale, granting them early access to new technologies and priority allocations during shortages. This advantage shields their production from supply chain disruptions, unlike smaller competitors like Sony or Panasonic. With increased revenues, they can invest more in R&D, developing products with unique selling points, thus perpetuating their growth cycle.
Price prediction
Investing in new technologies
According to Euromonitor International’s Voice of The Industry: Digital survey, companies are prioritizing investments in cloud, artificial intelligence (AI), and the Internet of Things (IoT) over the next five years. AI usage spans analytics, demand prediction, customer engagement, and technical troubleshooting. Ecosystem investments are focused on interoperability, while interest in the metaverse, partly sparked by Facebook's optimism, is driving AR/VR investments. Cloud technology investment remains strong due to its versatility, with emphasis on SaaS and PaaS models amid growing digital connectivity and remote work trends. Additionally, smart assistants in various devices are gaining traction, enhancing user engagement.
